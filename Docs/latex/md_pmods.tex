The Pmod Connection system is using a grid of 2 rows and 6 columns of pins.

It was designed by Digilent to offer something compatible with both classical jumper wires used on breadboards, and a standardised pinout that supports multiple protocols on the same connector.

This is popular on FPGA boards as it permits them to support any protocol over the same connector with the right pinout, rarely possible on MCUs.

This standard is documented \href{https://reference.digilentinc.com/_media/reference/pmod/pmodoledrgb/pmodoledrgb_sch.pdf}{\texttt{ here}}.\hypertarget{md_pmods_autotoc_md34}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Pmod in the pico-\/ice}{Pmod in the pico-\/ice}}\label{md_pmods_autotoc_md34}
Here is an \href{md_getting__started.html\#autotoc_md2}{\texttt{ example of how to solder}} the Pmod connectors.

There are 4 Pmod connectors in the pico-\/ice, numbered clockwise from 1 to 4 starting from the USB connector.


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item 2 connected ot the i\+CE40
\item 1 connected to both the RP2350 and i\+CE40
\item 1 connected to the RP2350
\end{DoxyItemize}

The pinout of the RP2350 is made to be compatible with SPI0, with the other protocols that may be bit-\/banged or implemented with PIO to be pin-\/compatible.

For the i\+CE40, the pin numbers are available from \href{https://github.com/tinyvision-ai-inc/pico-ice-sdk/blob/main/rtl/pico_ice.pcf}{\texttt{ pico\+\_\+ice.\+pcf}}.\hypertarget{md_pmods_autotoc_md35}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Pmod Modules}{Pmod Modules}}\label{md_pmods_autotoc_md35}
